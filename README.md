# CIPHER

CIPHER project repository

**CIPHER: Cardiovascular inequalities in English primary care**  
An electronic health records study focusing on the diagnosis and treatment of high blood pressure, high cholesterol, and the uptake and reach of the NHS Health Check.

CIPHER is a population-scale electronic health records study examining inequalities in the detection, treatment, and monitoring of cardiovascular risk factors, specifically high blood pressure and high cholesterol, across England.

## Overview

Cardiovascular disease (CVD) accounts for around 25% of deaths in England and is a major driver of health inequalities. Despite national prevention programmes such as **NHS Health Checks** and digital initiatives like **NHS BP@Home**, uptake and outcomes remain uneven across population groups.

This project investigates how inequalities arise across the full care pathway, from invitation and diagnosis to treatment and control, and how these patterns have changed over time.

## Aims

- Quantify inequalities in:
  - Detection, treatment, and monitoring of hypertension and hypercholesterolaemia  
  - Uptake and impact of NHS Health Checks and BP@Home  
- Identify underserved populations and gaps in care pathways  
- Assess trends over time and the impact of national initiatives  

## Data

- Primary care records  
- Secondary care data (SUS)  
- ONS mortality data  

**Population:** Adults (≥18 years) registered with a GP in England (2016–present)

## Methods

- Descriptive trend analyses  
- Regression modelling (logistic, Cox, Poisson/negative binomial)  
- Time series and interrupted time series analyses  

## Outputs & Impact

CIPHER will generate actionable evidence to:

- Support targeted, equitable CVD prevention  
- Inform NHS policy, commissioning, and service design  
- Identify where patients are "missed" along the care pathway  

Findings align with the **NHS Long Term Plan** and **Core20PLUS5** strategy, supporting a shift toward prevention, community care, and digital delivery.
